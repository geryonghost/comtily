Used Car Sales Ad Aggregator

Problem: The big car sites focus on profits and how to make thsoe profits. That relies on dealers and private sellers to pay to post their cars on the sites.

We plan to create a partnership with dealerships where they send us (or we retreive) basic data about a car which we use to create an unbiased listing. This way each listing looks and feels exactly the same and the data speaks for itself. The consumer can then choose to visit the dealership website accordingly.

I had this idea when searching for my vehicle. I had a very specific year, make, model, color, and options I wanted. Each site had different methods to search for this and it was hard. Some listings were on multiple sites, but look different. Some listings were only on one, but were missing important facts like photos or price. This didn't make me want to click into the dealers website, I simply skipped it. I did this for 6 months until I found the perfect match 1000 miles away. The dealership was excellent, but I bet there is an easier way. I also know that I am extreme, but would like to make it easier for everyone to pick their desired car and find it whereever it is.

Cyber Security for Small Business

Problem: Small Business can be affected by the same cyber criminals that big business can. However, the outcomes can be dramatically different. Most small businesses that are affected by cyber crime go out of business because it is too expensive to fix or their reputation is lost.

We plan to create a simple platform that is easy to understand, easy to install, and easy to mange for Small Businesses. The best part is if we keep profits out of it, the costs will not be astronomical for small businesses. At one point we had a good plan to keep automated security updates, anti-malware, training, and backups to a minimal cost. But as a small business ourselves (and not good at sales or marketing) we struggled to obtain enough customers to quit our day jobs.

Weather App

Problem: Weather apps are very good at relaying the most common information to us. However, they miss very key points that should be options like when does the daytime high occur or is it getting warmer or colder.

We would like to create a very simple application that works both on the web and phones/tablets that can be customized with information that others might not include or are hard to find. But front and cetner we want the current temp and whether or not the trend is going up or down. Here in northern Illinois knowing which direction the temperature is going is just as important as what it is. I don't want to look at the hourly forecasts for this simple information. Plus if it is 8 AM and the temperature is at its highest I want to know that without have to search for that.

Auto Insurance

Problem: There are a limited number of auto insurance companies that are not built for investor profit that cater to the standard consumer. This makes profits the priority over the well being of the consumer. Think Bob Par from the Incredibles that does whatever it takes to help his customers and gets fired.

We would like to determine the feasibility to run a not for profit auto insurance company to meet the needs of the consumer. We know people that are being abused by the system. "A single parent with a teen driver and only one car paying more than a family with five cars and two teenagers." Another example is "A retiree just under 70 paying more on a single car." We would like to be super transparent and let the consumer know exactly why they have the rate they have. Not simple and generic line items like "premium" and "discounts."
